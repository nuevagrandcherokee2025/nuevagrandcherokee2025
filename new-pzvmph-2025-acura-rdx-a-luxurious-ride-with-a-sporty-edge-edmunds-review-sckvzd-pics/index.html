2025 Acura RDX: A Luxurious Ride With A Sporty Edge (Edmunds Review)

2025 Acura RDX: A Luxurious Ride with a Sporty Edge (Edmunds Review)

The 2025 Acura RDX, a compact luxury SUV, continues its reign as a popular choice for drivers seeking a balance of style, performance, and technology. With subtle refinements and a commitment to delivering a premium driving experience, the RDX remains a strong contender in its segment. Let’s delve into what makes this vehicle stand out, exploring its strengths, weaknesses, and overall value proposition.

Design: A Symphony of Elegance and Sportiness

The 2025 RDX retains its signature Acura design language, characterized by a sharp, angular aesthetic that exudes both elegance and sportiness. The front fascia features Acura’s signature "Diamond Pentagon" grille, flanked by sharp LED headlights and a sculpted hood. The side profile showcases a sleek silhouette with sculpted lines that flow towards the rear, while the rear end boasts integrated taillights and a prominent diffuser. The overall design is modern and sophisticated, making a strong statement on the road.

Acura offers a range of exterior colors, including premium metallic options, allowing buyers to personalize their RDX. The available wheel designs, ranging from 18 to 20 inches, further enhance the vehicle’s visual appeal.

Interior: Luxury Meets Functionality

Stepping inside the RDX reveals a cabin that prioritizes both luxury and practicality. The dashboard is driver-focused, with a clean layout and high-quality materials. The use of soft-touch surfaces, leather upholstery, and metallic accents creates a premium ambiance.

The front seats are comfortable and supportive, offering ample adjustability for optimal driving posture. The rear seats provide ample legroom and headroom for passengers, even taller adults. The cargo area is also spacious, offering a generous 29.5 cubic feet of space behind the rear seats, which can be expanded to 58.9 cubic feet by folding the seats down.

Technology: A Seamless Blend of Innovation and Convenience

The 2025 RDX is loaded with advanced technology features that enhance the driving experience and provide a seamless connection to the digital world. The centerpiece of the cabin is the 10.2-inch touchscreen infotainment system, which boasts a responsive interface and intuitive navigation. Apple CarPlay and Android Auto integration are standard, allowing drivers to seamlessly connect their smartphones for hands-free calling, music streaming, and navigation.

Acura’s ELS Studio 3D premium audio system is available, delivering a truly immersive listening experience. The system features 16 speakers strategically placed throughout the cabin, creating a concert-like atmosphere.

Performance: A Balanced and Engaging Driving Experience

The 2025 RDX is powered by a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine that generates 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. This engine provides ample power for everyday driving and offers a satisfying surge of acceleration when needed. Power is delivered to the front wheels through a 10-speed automatic transmission, which shifts smoothly and efficiently.

The RDX offers a choice between front-wheel drive and Acura’s Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) system. SH-AWD provides enhanced traction and stability in challenging conditions, making the RDX a capable vehicle in all seasons.

Handling and Ride: A Blend of Comfort and Agility

The RDX’s suspension is well-tuned, offering a balance of comfort and agility. The ride is smooth and compliant over most surfaces, absorbing bumps and potholes with ease. However, the RDX doesn’t sacrifice handling for comfort. The steering is precise and responsive, allowing drivers to confidently navigate corners.

Safety: A Comprehensive Suite of Features

Acura prioritizes safety in the RDX, equipping it with a comprehensive suite of advanced safety features. The AcuraWatch suite of driver-assist technologies includes features such as:

  • Adaptive Cruise Control: Maintains a safe distance from the vehicle ahead.
  • Lane Keeping Assist: Helps keep the vehicle centered in its lane.
  • Road Departure Mitigation: Detects when the vehicle is drifting out of its lane and provides corrective steering input.
  • Collision Mitigation Braking System: Detects potential collisions and applies the brakes to avoid or mitigate the impact.

Fuel Economy: Efficient Performance

The 2025 RDX delivers respectable fuel economy for a luxury SUV. The front-wheel drive model achieves an EPA-estimated 24 mpg city, 30 mpg highway, and 27 mpg combined. The SH-AWD model gets slightly lower fuel economy ratings at 23 mpg city, 29 mpg highway, and 26 mpg combined.

Pricing and Value: A Competitive Offering

The 2025 Acura RDX starts at around $40,000 for the base model. This price point positions it competitively within the compact luxury SUV segment. Higher trim levels, such as the Technology and A-Spec packages, offer additional features and amenities, increasing the price accordingly.

Pros:

  • Stylish and sophisticated design
  • Luxurious and comfortable interior
  • Powerful and efficient engine
  • Balanced handling and ride quality
  • Comprehensive safety features
  • Competitive pricing

Cons:

  • Infotainment system can be slow at times
  • Rear seats can be tight for taller adults
  • Some competitors offer more cargo space

Conclusion: A Winning Formula

The 2025 Acura RDX remains a compelling choice in the compact luxury SUV segment. Its stylish design, luxurious interior, powerful engine, and comprehensive safety features make it a well-rounded and desirable vehicle. While it has some minor drawbacks, such as a somewhat sluggish infotainment system and limited rear seat space, the RDX’s overall value proposition is strong, making it a worthy contender for those seeking a blend of luxury, performance, and practicality.

Beyond the Review: Exploring the RDX’s Potential

While the 2025 RDX offers a compelling package, it’s important to consider its potential for future development. The automotive industry is constantly evolving, and Acura could further enhance the RDX by:

  • Improving Infotainment System Responsiveness: Addressing the occasional sluggishness of the infotainment system would enhance the user experience.
  • Expanding Cargo Space: Increasing the cargo space, particularly behind the rear seats, would make the RDX even more practical for families and those who frequently haul cargo.
  • Introducing a Hybrid Powertrain: Offering a hybrid powertrain option would appeal to environmentally conscious buyers and improve fuel efficiency.
  • Expanding Driver-Assist Technologies: Adding features like automated parking assist and traffic jam assist would further enhance safety and convenience.

By addressing these areas, Acura could solidify the RDX’s position as a top contender in the competitive compact luxury SUV market. The RDX has the potential to be a truly exceptional vehicle, and these improvements would help it reach its full potential.

Ultimately, the 2025 Acura RDX is a well-rounded and stylish SUV that offers a compelling mix of luxury, performance, and technology. While it has a few areas for improvement, it remains a worthy contender in its segment and a strong choice for drivers seeking a premium driving experience.
